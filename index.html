I am currently a Postdoctoral Reseach Associate at Princeton University working with Prof. John D. Storey.

I received my PhD degree in Statistics in December 2012 from Purdue University under the supervision of Prof. Rebecca W. Doerge, a Master's degree in Mathematics in May 2009 from University of Hawaii at Manoa under the supervision of Prof. L. Thomas Ramsey, and a Master's degree in Mathematics in July 2006 from Sichuan Univresity in China under the supervision of Prof. Changlin Cai (who had retired).

I have broad interests in statistics and mathematics. I have been mostly working on simultaneous inference for high-dimensional dependent data via latent models and for high-dimensional, low sample size discrete data. I have also worked on time series models, Ito diffusion processes, and natural exponential families using analytic and meromorphic function theories. Recently, I have become interested in graphical models, analysis of non-Euclidean data and random matrix theory.
Here is my cv. For completed projects by topics, please click on research; for some details on completed projects, please click on research document.

I am currently working on

  • Simultaneous inference under endogeneity

  • Modelling and analysis of non-Euclidean data such as medical images

  • Graphical models for mixed data

  • Testing and identifying structures in high-dimensional data

But I am also working on projects in mathematics

  • Relationship between geometry of mainfolds and statistical inference

  • Classification of probability measures compatible with a graphcial structure

  • Spectral property of random matrices with dependent entries and its applications to statistics
